Getting Started with HackTheBox

These steps will guide you from creating an account to launching your first machine in the HackTheBox Labs environment.

Create an Account

Visit the HackTheBox site and sign up for a free account. Confirm your email and complete any onboarding steps required by HTB.

Go to HackTheBox

Choose Labs or Machines

From the HTB dashboard select either the Labs (networked environments) or the Machines (individual boxes). Labs provide a simulated network you can connect to; Machines are standalone targets.

Set Up Your VPN (if required)

Many HTB labs and retired machines require a VPN connection. Download the connection pack from HTB and follow their instructions to configure OpenVPN or the official client. Only connect to HTB network endpoints provided by HTB.

Scan the Target

Run reconnaissance to discover open ports and services. A typical first pass includes an nmap scan. Example command to enumerate ports and services:

nmap -sC -sV -oA nmap/initial 10.10.10.123

Replace the IP with the machine's IP shown in the HTB interface.

Enumerate and Research

Investigate discovered services, banner text, and versions. Search for known CVEs, default credentials, or misconfigurations. Use safe, non-destructive techniques while learning.

Exploit and Escalate

When you find a plausible path, attempt to gain initial access using appropriate tools or manual techniques. After initial access, work on privilege escalation to capture the root or administrator flag.

Document and Learn

Keep notes as you progress. Record commands, outputs, and why a step succeeded or failed. This habit accelerates learning and makes writeups easier later.

Ethics & Rules

Only practice on machines and labs you are authorized to access. Do not scan or attack systems without explicit permission. Respect HTB's rules and community guidelines.

Ready to try a machine?
