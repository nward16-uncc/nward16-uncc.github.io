Machines

Short, entry-level descriptions of the Tier 1 (Starting Point) machines so you can choose which to try next.

NOTE: Although most machines are marked as easy or very easy, they are actually very hard for a beginner. Be patient.

Appointment

Difficulty: Very Easy | OS: Linux

Overview: Appointment is designed to teach basic service enumeration and simple foothold techniques. Expect an approachable, guided flow ideal for first-time machine users.

Skills practiced: basic network scanning, service probing, reading simple config/files.

Why pick it: Very beginner-friendly and a gentle introduction to the HTB workflow.

Sequel

Difficulty: Very Easy | OS: Linux

Overview: Sequel focuses on straightforward reconnaissance and following obvious leads from discovered services to gain access.

Skills practiced: web and service enumeration, interpreting banners and version info.

Why pick it: Great for practicing a methodical scan → enumerate → exploit cycle without complex pivots.

Crocodile

Difficulty: Very Easy | OS: Linux

Overview: Crocodile introduces simple web and file-discovery techniques that lead to a clear foothold.

Skills practiced: directory discovery, file analysis, using discovered artifacts to progress.

Why pick it: Teaches essential web enumeration habits and reading clues left in files.

Ignition

Difficulty: Very Easy | OS: Linux

Overview: Ignition is aimed at teaching straightforward vulnerability discovery and safe verification of exploits.

Skills practiced: targeted scanning, manual verification of potential issues, cautious exploitation.

Why pick it: Good next step for learners who want to practice validating findings safely.

Pennyworth

Difficulty: Very Easy | OS: Linux

Overview: Pennyworth emphasizes following a small chain of clues—from an exposed service to useful information that leads to a shell.

Skills practiced: service interaction, credential discovery, initial foothold techniques.

Why pick it: Encourages careful enumeration and correlating small pieces of information into a working attack path.

Tactics

Difficulty: Very Easy | OS: Linux

Overview: Tactics teaches basic problem-solving on simple services and configurations; expect clear, teachable steps.

Skills practiced: reconnaissance, interpreting service responses, basic privilege escalation checks.

Why pick it: Excellent for reinforcing the methodology you learned on earlier Starting Point boxes.
