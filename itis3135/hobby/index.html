What Is Cybersecurity?

Many people have heard the term "cybersecurity" used before, but they aren't quite sure what it means.

Cybersecurity is the practice of protecting data, devices, networks, and people from potential cyber threats.

Key Areas of Cybersecurity
Field Focus Example Activities
Network Security Protecting communication between systems Configuring firewalls, monitoring traffic
Application Security Securing software and web apps Code reviews, penetration testing
Digital Forensics Investigating and analyzing breaches Recovering evidence from systems

Who Am I?

My name is Nathaniel Ward. I'm a student at UNC Charlotte majoring in Computer Science with a concentration in cybersecurity.

Ever since I had access to a computer, I enjoyed diving into its internals to figure out how they work exactly and how I can make them do something that they weren't intended to do.

I am always doing my best to engage in this hobby in the most ethical way possible. Working with potentially destructive programs requires many precautions that must be taken, and I am sure to always take them.

When Do I Practice Cybersecurity?

The beauty of this hobby is that you can generally practice anytime of the year and anytime of the day. Personally, my favorite time is mid-day when the sun is overhead.

There are certain times of the year when there is a cybersecurity convention or tournament in town, which is another great time to expand your skills. There is actually one on November 1st here on campus.





Where Do I Learn Cybersecurity?

I typically practice my hobby at my desk, at home. Sometimes I take my laptop outside and practice there.

On campus there are multiple clubs and areas where practicing this kind of hobby is encouraged, so that is always an option as well.

How Do I Explore Cybersecurity?

I have my own lab setup that I built from scratch at home. I have multiple laptops and a single board computer called a Raspberry Pi that functions as a small server.

On top of that, I have created virtual machines and networks so that I can simulate real world networks.

There are many tools and reading materials that I use to learn. Cybersecurity requires a LOT of reading to stay on top of current trends.

Why Do I Enjoy Cybersecurity?

I enjoy cybersecurity because it's so complex and you can dive down a rabbit hole that seemingly never ends. It feels like you are unraveling a mystery and it's very satisfying when you figure it out after expending lots of time & effort.

I've always had a very focused interest in computers in general, so learning how they work and how to make them behave in different ways is very enjoyable to me.

Challenging hobbies always seem to have the biggest "payoff" in my mind. To me, a hobby isn't about relaxing or unwinding, it's about the thrill of completing the challenge.
