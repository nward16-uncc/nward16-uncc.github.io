Website Evaluations

The FWA (Favorite Website Awards)

The FWA is an award site that showcases innovative and experimental websites from designers and developers around the world. It balances creativity with usability, making it a strong example of an artsy site that still respects design principles.

  • Contrast: The site uses bold typography, vibrant imagery, and high contrast between text and backgrounds to highlight featured projects.
  • Repetition: Project previews follow a consistent card format, reinforcing a unified browsing experience across the site.
  • Alignment: Despite featuring experimental designs, the site itself stays aligned to a grid structure that keeps navigation clean and logical.
  • Proximity: Each projectâ€™s visuals, title, and designer credits are grouped together, making it easy for users to connect content with its creator.

Zombo.com

Zombo.com is an intentionally absurd website. It features almost no content, just looping audio and a simple animated home page. It has become a classic example of poor design.

  • Contrast: Minimal contrast, especially on the animation, with bland colors that fail to emphasize any important elements.
  • Repetition: There is almost no repetition or consistency, since the entire site is basically one looping screen.
  • Alignment: No meaningful layout exists; the elements appear randomly centered with no design structure.
  • Proximity: With so little actual content, there is no clear grouping of information or logical organization.
